Shahs of Kabob

Shahs of Kabob is another Persian restaurant in Miami. They have a location in South Miami near University of Miami, and have expanded to Doral which is west of Miami. This is a more casual place or somewhere to get take-out from. They focus on the basics: popular appetizers, meats, salads, and rice. They add a twist on their menu by providing wraps which are not traditional but a fun addition. They are great for catering as well; I have had it twice for catering and it’s perfect for sharing with a large group of people. I like to get their combination platter of chicken (joojeh) and beef (kubideh) kabob with rice and shirazi salad. It can range from $12-20 a person. Kababi Cafe

Kababi Cafe is in Plantation, Florida which is north of Miami closer to Fort Lauderdale. There’s two different ways to eat here. The first way is ordering individual dishes or family style. The other way is at certain times of day, they have a buffet of everything people tend to order. This place is good for the basics: meats, rice, salads, stews, breads, and desserts. It’s a pretty traditional restaurant but they are good at the staples. They also have performers and musicians on Friday and Saturday which makes it a fun place to go with a group. I am not 100% sure how much the buffet costs per person but either way, it should be about $30 a person.
